Dear Potential Participant,

You are invited to participate in a research study about Human-Robot Interaction conducted by Lingheng Meng. It will be used as part of Lingheng's PhD study. This project is supervised by Dr. Dana Kulić at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering and Dr. Rob Gorbet at the Department of Knowledge Integration at the University of Waterloo, Canada. This study aims to assess if engaging behavior can be learned from user preferences for one of two video clips by asking user which one of two video clips he/she prefers for large-scale interactive sculpture aiming to engage interaction for a long time.

Study details: If you decide to participate, you will be asked to complete a questionnaire via an online survey about your experience of interacting with the sculpture Meander. You may decline to answer any question(s) you prefer not to answer. Prior to taking the questionnaire you will be asked to interact with Meander for at least 5 minutes. The questionnaire itself will take approximately 3 minutes. There are no known or anticipated risks to participation in this study. Your participation in this study is voluntary and anonymous.

Location and Date: If you wish to interact with the sculpture, the sculpture is locate at Tapestry Hall, 74 Grand Ave. Cambridge, Ontario, and it is available for public viewing and interaction at any work time of the Tapestry Hall posted on their Facebook page https://www.facebook.com/tapestryhall. The survey will take place online at https://las-ai.uk.r.appspot.com. The survey is available anytime from [XXX date placeholder will be filled after approved] to [XXX date placeholder will be filled after approved].

Eligibility Requirements for Participation: Potential participants must be over 18 to participate.

Confidentiality and Data Retention: No names or other identifying information will be collected on the surveys. While aggregated results may be used in theses or publications, only researchers will have access to the data. Your name will not appear in any publication resulting from this study. However, with your permission, anonymous quotations may be used in the thesis or any publications. You will be completing the study by an online survey deployed on Google Cloud. When information is transmitted over the internet privacy cannot be guaranteed. There is always a risk your responses may be intercepted by a third party (e.g., government agencies, hackers). University of Waterloo researchers will not collect or use internet protocol (IP) addresses or other information which could link your participation to your computer or electronic device without first informing you. The data collected from this study will be downloaded from Google Cloud and maintained on a password protected, disk encrypted laptop. All the data will be electronically archived after completion of the study and maintained for a minimum of 8 years. Once your data is submitted, you cannot withdraw your consent to participate as the researchers have no way of identifying which data is yours.

Ethic Review and Clearance: This study has been reviewed and received ethics clearance through a University of Waterloo Research Ethics Committee (ORE#42436). If you have questions for the Committee contact the Office of Research Ethics, at 1-519-888-4567 ext. 36005 or ohrac@uwaterloo.ca.

Questions and Contact: If you have any questions or would like additional information regarding this study, please contact Lingheng via email. Also, if you prefer, you may contact my faculty supervisors Dr. Dana Kulić and Dr. Rob Gorbet, who are professors at the University of Waterloo, via their emails and/or phone numbers.
