Generate Engaging Behavior from Human Preferences

Tapestry Hall, 74 Grand Ave. Cambridge, Ontario.

[XXX date placeholder], 2021 - [XXX date placeholder], 2021

Meander is a large-scale testbed environment constructed within a historic warehouse building at the center of a residential high-rise development in Cambridge, Ontario. It's also a research platform we use to study how people interact with it, and whether it can use Artificial Intelligence (AI) technology to learn engaging behavior from its visitors. In this study, we specifically investigate if engaging behavior can be learned from human preferences by employing Preference Learning and Reinforcement Learning techniques.

From the hardware perspective, sensors embedded within the environment signal the presence of occupants, and actuators send ripples of light, motion and sound through the system in response. From the software perspective, the behavior of the sculpture is hierarchically controlled by a lower level of behavior-engine and a higher level of AI agent which continuously incorporates human preference into its behavior policy to maximize the perceived engagement of its visitors.

How This Works

  • You can choose one of or both the surveys listed in Active Surveys.
  • There will be no researcher on site, so do not worry about face-to-face contact with researchers. If you wish to participate in the surveys after interacting with the sculpture that you can complete the survey on your phone or other electronic devices.
  • For any question, please feel free to check FAQs, ask in the right bottom chat box, or contact Lingheng at lingheng.meng@uwaterloo.ca.
